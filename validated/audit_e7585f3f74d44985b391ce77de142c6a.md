### Title
ERC20 Blocklisting of Destination Recipient Permanently Freezes Bridged Funds â€” (`evm/src/omni-bridge/contracts/OmniBridge.sol`, `starknet/src/omni_bridge.cairo`)

---

### Summary

When a user bridges tokens from NEAR to EVM (or StarkNet), the source-chain tokens are burned or locked on NEAR at `initTransfer` time. If the ERC20 token on the destination chain implements address blocklisting (e.g., USDC, USDT) and the recipient address is blocklisted, `finTransfer` will always revert. Because there is no cancel or refund path on NEAR, the user's funds are permanently and irrecoverably frozen.

---

### Finding Description

**EVM â€” `OmniBridge.finTransfer`**

In `evm/src/omni-bridge/contracts/OmniBridge.sol`, the `finTransfer` function executes the following sequence:

1. Check nonce not used (line 283)
2. Mark nonce used: `completedTransfers[payload.destinationNonce] = true` (line 287)

4. Transfer tokens to recipient â€” for native ERC20s: `IERC20(payload.tokenAddress).safeTransfer(payload.recipient, payload.amount)` (lines 351â€“354)

If `safeTransfer` reverts (e.g., because `payload.recipient` is blocklisted by USDC/USDT), the entire transaction reverts â€” including the nonce marking. The nonce is therefore not permanently consumed, but the `FinTransfer` event is **never emitted**. Since the NEAR side requires a proof of this event to finalize the transfer (via `claim_fee`), the transfer can never be settled.

**StarkNet â€” `omni_bridge.fin_transfer`**

If the ERC20 `transfer` returns `false` (blocklisted recipient), the `assert` panics, the entire transaction reverts, and the `FinTransfer` event is never emitted.

**No recovery path on NEAR**

On NEAR, when a user initiates a NEARâ†’EVM transfer, tokens are burned/locked and the transfer is stored in `pending_transfers`. The only way to remove a transfer from `pending_transfers` is via `claim_fee_callback`, which requires a proof of a `FinTransfer` event from EVM: [4](#0-3) 

Since `finTransfer` on EVM always reverts for a blocklisted recipient, no `FinTransfer` event is ever emitted, `claim_fee` can never be called, and the burned/locked tokens on NEAR are permanently irrecoverable. There is no `cancel_transfer`, `refund_transfer`, or admin rescue function in the NEAR bridge contract. The MPC signature covers the recipient address, so no re-routing to a different recipient is possible without a protocol-level intervention.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical â€” Permanent freezing / irrecoverable lock of user funds in bridge flows.**

A user who initiates a NEARâ†’EVM (or NEARâ†’StarkNet) transfer of a blocklist-capable token (USDC, USDT) to a recipient that is or becomes blocklisted will have their source-chain tokens permanently destroyed (burned bridged tokens) or permanently locked (native tokens held by the bridge). There is no on-chain recovery path.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium-High.** USDC and USDT are among the most commonly bridged stablecoins and both implement address blocklisting. Blocklisting can occur due to regulatory action, sanctions compliance, or exchange-reported theft â€” all of which are real-world events. A user who is already blocklisted on EVM can still initiate a transfer from NEAR (the NEAR bridge has no visibility into EVM blocklist state). Additionally, a user's EVM address could be blocklisted *after* they initiate a transfer from NEAR but *before* the relayer calls `finTransfer` on EVM. No attacker capability beyond initiating a standard bridge transfer is required for self-harm; a third party with influence over the ERC20 issuer could also grief any user.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Separate token delivery from transfer finalization.** Mark the transfer as finalized and emit the `FinTransfer` event unconditionally after signature verification. Store the owed amount in a claimable balance mapping. Let the recipient (or anyone on their behalf) pull the tokens in a separate `claimTokens` call. This way, a blocklisted recipient does not prevent finalization, and the NEAR side can settle the transfer regardless.

2. **Alternatively, use a non-reverting transfer helper.** Wrap the ERC20 transfer in a try/catch (EVM) or safe-dispatcher (StarkNet). On failure, credit the amount to a per-recipient escrow mapping and emit the `FinTransfer` event so the NEAR side can settle. Provide a separate `rescueTransfer` function allowing the recipient (or an admin) to redirect funds to an unblocklisted address.

3. **Add a NEAR-side cancel/refund path.** Implement a DAO-gated `cancel_transfer` on NEAR that can refund burned/locked tokens when a destination-chain finalization is provably impossible, as a last-resort recovery mechanism.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:** Alice holds USDC on NEAR. She initiates a NEARâ†’EVM bridge transfer of 1000 USDC to her EVM address `0xAlice`.

1. Alice calls `ft_transfer_call` on the NEAR USDC token contract. The NEAR bridge burns 1000 USDC and stores the `TransferMessage` in `pending_transfers`.
2. Before the relayer calls `finTransfer` on EVM, USDC issuer blocklists `0xAlice` (e.g., due to a regulatory freeze).
3. Relayer calls `OmniBridge.finTransfer(signature, payload)` on EVM. Execution reaches line 351:
   ```solidity
   IERC20(payload.tokenAddress).safeTransfer(payload.recipient, payload.amount);
   // USDC.transfer(0xAlice, 1000e6) â†’ reverts: "Blacklisted"
   ```
   The entire transaction reverts. `completedTransfers[nonce]` is reset to `false`. No `FinTransfer` event is emitted.
4. Every subsequent `finTransfer` attempt reverts identically.
5. On NEAR, `claim_fee` requires a proof of a `FinTransfer` event from EVM. Since no such event exists, `claim_fee_callback` â†’ `remove_transfer_message` is never reached.
6. Alice's 1000 USDC remain permanently burned on NEAR. The `pending_transfers` entry is never cleared. Funds are irrecoverably lost. [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)
