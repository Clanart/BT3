### Title
Pending Transfer with Non-Zero Fee Becomes Permanently Irrecoverable When `amount_without_fee` Normalizes to Zero on Destination Chain — (File: `near/omni-bridge/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

When a NEAR-side transfer is initiated with a non-zero fee, the transfer is stored in `pending_transfers`. If the `amount_without_fee` (amount minus fee) is too small to produce a non-zero value after decimal normalization to the destination chain's precision, `sign_transfer` will always revert with `BridgeError::InvalidAmountToTransfer`. Because there is no cancel or refund mechanism for pending transfers, and the fee can only be increased (never decreased) via `update_transfer_fee`, the user's tokens are permanently locked in the bridge with no recovery path.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Transfer creation with no normalization guard.**

`init_transfer` (called from `ft_on_transfer`) stores the transfer in `pending_transfers` after only checking that `fee < amount`: [1](#0-0) 

There is no check that `amount - fee` will produce a non-zero value after decimal normalization to the destination chain. For tokens whose NEAR representation uses more decimals than the destination chain (e.g., 24 NEAR decimals vs. 6 EVM decimals), any `amount_without_fee < 10^(origin_decimals − dest_decimals)` normalizes to zero via floor division.

**Step 2 — `sign_transfer` hard-reverts when normalized amount is zero.**

`sign_transfer` computes the normalized amount and enforces it is positive: [2](#0-1) 

If `amount_to_transfer == 0`, the entire transaction panics. The transfer remains in `pending_transfers` unchanged.

**Step 3 — No cancel or refund path exists.**

The only ways a transfer is removed from `pending_transfers` are:

- `sign_transfer_callback` when `fee.is_zero()` — inapplicable here because fee is non-zero: [3](#0-2) 

- `claim_fee_callback` after a proof of finalization on the destination chain is submitted — impossible because the transfer was never signed and never executed on the destination chain: [4](#0-3) 

There is no `cancel_transfer`, `refund_transfer`, or any admin function to remove an arbitrary entry from `pending_transfers`.

**Step 4 — Fee update cannot rescue the transfer.**

`update_transfer_fee` enforces that the new fee is ≥ the current fee: [5](#0-4) 

The sender cannot decrease the fee to make `amount_without_fee` larger. The transfer is irrecoverably stuck.

**Step 5 — Decimal normalization is confirmed to use floor division.**

The comment in `claim_fee_callback` explicitly states: [6](#0-5) 

This confirms `normalize(x)` can be strictly less than `x` and, for sufficiently small `x`, equals zero.

---

### Impact Explanation

The user's tokens are burned (for bridge tokens) or transferred to the bridge contract (for native tokens) at `init_transfer` time. Once the transfer enters the stuck state, those tokens are permanently unrecoverable: `sign_transfer` always reverts, `claim_fee` has no valid proof to submit, and no cancel/refund function exists. This constitutes **permanent freezing of user funds in the bridge flow**, matching the Critical impact class.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The scenario is reachable by any unprivileged user who:

1. Transfers a token whose NEAR decimal representation exceeds the destination chain's decimals (e.g., a NEAR-native token with 24 decimals bridged to an EVM chain where it is registered with 6 decimals).
2. Sets a non-zero fee such that `amount - fee < 10^(origin_decimals − dest_decimals)`.

This is a realistic combination: the fee validity check (`fee < amount`) does not prevent it, and the decimal gap of 18 (24 vs. 6) is common for NEAR-originated tokens. A user who sets a fee of, say, `amount - 1` (still valid per the check) on a small transfer will trigger this permanently.

---

### Recommendation

Add a normalization guard at `init_transfer` time (or at the point the transfer is stored) to verify that `normalize_amount(amount_without_fee, decimals) > 0`. Additionally, introduce a `cancel_transfer` function that allows the original sender to reclaim their tokens and remove the entry from `pending_transfers`, analogous to how the M-07 report recommends adding a health-factor check to enable liquidation of otherwise-stuck positions.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Register a token with `origin_decimals = 24`, `dest_decimals = 6` (decimal gap = 18).
2. Call `ft_transfer_call` on the token contract with:
   - `amount = 2` (2 yocto-units in 24-decimal representation)
   - `fee = 1` (valid: `fee < amount`)
   - `recipient` = any EVM address
3. `amount_without_fee = 1`. `normalize_amount(1, Decimals { decimals: 6, origin_decimals: 24 }) = 1 / 10^18 = 0` (floor division).
4. The transfer is stored in `pending_transfers`. The 2 yocto-units of the token are burned/locked.
5. Any call to `sign_transfer` for this `transfer_id` panics with `BridgeError::InvalidAmountToTransfer`.
6. `claim_fee` cannot be called (no destination-chain finalization proof exists).
7. `update_transfer_fee` can only increase the fee, making the situation worse.
8. The 2 yocto-units are permanently locked with no recovery path.
