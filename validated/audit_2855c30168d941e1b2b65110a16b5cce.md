### Title
MPC Signer Address Update Permanently Freezes In-Flight Zero-Fee Transfers - (File: `evm/src/omni-bridge/contracts/OmniBridge.sol`)

---

### Summary

`OmniBridge.sol` stores `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` as a mutable global state variable that is read at `finTransfer()` time to verify the MPC signature. The NEAR-side `sign_transfer_callback` removes zero-fee transfer messages from `pending_transfers` immediately after a successful MPC signing. If the admin legitimately rotates the MPC key (calls `setNearBridgeDerivedAddress()`) between the NEAR-side signing and the EVM-side finalization, the already-produced signature becomes invalid on EVM, and the transfer cannot be re-signed because it was already removed from NEAR state. User funds are permanently frozen.

---

### Finding Description

`OmniBridge.sol` declares `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` as a plain mutable storage variable: [1](#0-0) 

It is updated at any time by the admin: [2](#0-1) 

At `finTransfer()` execution time, the current (post-update) value is used to verify the signature: [3](#0-2) 

On the NEAR side, `sign_transfer_callback` removes the pending transfer from storage immediately after a successful MPC signing **if the fee is zero**: [4](#0-3) 

Because the transfer is deleted from `pending_transfers` at that point, there is no way to call `sign_transfer` again to produce a fresh signature under the new key. The `destination_nonce` embedded in the already-signed payload is also already consumed from NEAR's perspective, so no recovery path exists.

The same `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` is also read at `deployToken()` time: [5](#0-4) 

meaning a key rotation also invalidates any in-flight token-deployment signatures.

The root cause is structurally identical to the referenced report: a global configurable parameter (`nearBridgeDerivedAddress` here, `LOAN_TERM` there) is read at settlement time rather than being captured and stored at the moment the commitment is made.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Permanent freezing of user funds.**

A user who initiated a zero-fee NEAR→EVM transfer has already burned or locked their tokens on the source chain at `init_transfer` time. After the MPC signs the payload and the transfer is removed from `pending_transfers`, if `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` is updated before `finTransfer` is called on EVM:

- The EVM call reverts with `InvalidSignature`.
- The transfer no longer exists in NEAR `pending_transfers`, so it cannot be re-signed.
- The `completedTransfers` nonce on EVM is not set (the call reverted), but the NEAR-side record is gone.
- The user has no recourse: tokens are burned/locked on NEAR and will never be released on EVM.

This satisfies the allowed impact: *"Permanent freezing, irrecoverable lock, or unclaimable settlement of user or protocol funds in bridge, token, or vault flows."*

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Key rotation of the MPC-derived address is a standard operational procedure (e.g., MPC service upgrade, key compromise response, bridge migration). The window of vulnerability is the latency between NEAR-side signing and EVM-side finalization — typically seconds to minutes, but potentially longer under network congestion or relayer downtime. Any zero-fee transfer in flight during a key rotation is permanently lost. Given that zero-fee transfers are a normal user choice and key rotations are expected lifecycle events, the likelihood is realistic.

---

### Recommendation

Store the `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` that was active at the time the MPC signature was produced inside the signed payload itself, or alternatively record it in the `TransferMessagePayload` on NEAR so the EVM contract can verify against the address that actually signed rather than the current global value.

A simpler mitigation: before calling `setNearBridgeDerivedAddress`, enforce a time-lock or a drain period during which no new transfers are signed and all in-flight signed transfers are finalized (or explicitly cancelled with a refund path).

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice calls `ft_on_transfer` → `init_transfer` on NEAR with `fee = 0`. Her tokens are locked. Transfer stored in `pending_transfers`.
2. Relayer calls `sign_transfer`. MPC produces a valid signature over a payload that embeds the current `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` (old key). `sign_transfer_callback` fires, sees `fee.is_zero() == true`, and **removes the transfer from `pending_transfers`**.
3. Admin calls `setNearBridgeDerivedAddress(newAddress)` (legitimate key rotation).
4. Relayer submits `finTransfer(signatureData, payload)` on EVM. `ECDSA.recover(hashed, signatureData)` returns `oldAddress`. `oldAddress != nearBridgeDerivedAddress` (which is now `newAddress`). Transaction reverts with `InvalidSignature`.
5. Alice's tokens are burned on NEAR (step 1), the transfer is gone from NEAR state (step 2), and the EVM nonce is not marked used (step 4). Alice's funds are permanently frozen with no recovery path.
