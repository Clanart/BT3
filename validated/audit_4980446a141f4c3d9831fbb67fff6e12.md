### Title
Resigned or Revoked Relayer Cannot Claim Earned Fees Due to Current-Status `#[trusted_relayer]` Guard on `claim_fee` — (File: `near/omni-bridge/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

`claim_fee` is gated by `#[trusted_relayer]`, which checks the caller's **current** trusted status at call time. A relayer who earned fees while trusted but subsequently resigned or was revoked by the DAO cannot call `claim_fee` for those already-earned fees. The fee tokens remain permanently locked inside the bridge contract with no recovery path.

---

### Finding Description

The `claim_fee` entry point is decorated with `#[trusted_relayer]`:

```rust
// near/omni-bridge/src/lib.rs  lines 1054-1064
#[payable]
#[trusted_relayer]
#[pause(except(roles(Role::DAO)))]
pub fn claim_fee(&mut self, #[serializer(borsh)] args: ClaimFeeArgs) -> Promise {
    self.verify_proof(args.chain_kind, args.prover_args).then(
        Self::ext(env::current_account_id())
            .with_attached_deposit(env::attached_deposit())
            .with_static_gas(CLAIM_FEE_CALLBACK_GAS)
            .claim_fee_callback(&env::predecessor_account_id()),
    )
}
```

The `#[trusted_relayer]` macro (declared at lines 245-249) checks `is_trusted_relayer` against the **current** relayer registry. It does not distinguish between "currently trusted" and "was trusted when the fee was earned."

The protocol provides two ways to exit the trusted-relayer set:

1. **Voluntary resignation** — `resign_trusted_relayer` removes the relayer and returns their stake.
2. **DAO revocation** — `reject_relayer_application` removes an active relayer and transfers their stake to the DAO.

After either event, `is_trusted_relayer` returns `false` for that account, and the `#[trusted_relayer]` guard rejects any subsequent `claim_fee` call from that account.

The fee is stored inside the `pending_transfers` map as part of the `TransferMessage`. For NEAR → Foreign transfers with a non-zero fee, `remove_transfer_message` is called **only** inside `claim_fee_callback` (line 1094). There is no alternative path to remove the entry or release the fee tokens. The `transfer_token_as_dao` escape hatch can move tokens but does not remove the `pending_transfers` entry or correct `locked_tokens` accounting, leaving the contract in a broken state.

The `claim_fee_callback` already enforces that only the designated `fee_recipient` can collect the fee (line 1083-1086):

```rust
require!(
    fee_recipient == *predecessor_account_id,
    BridgeError::OnlyFeeRecipientCanClaim.as_ref()
);
```

This means no other trusted relayer or DAO member can claim the fee on behalf of the resigned relayer, because the proof binds the fee to a specific `fee_recipient` address.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Permanent freezing of bridge funds.** When a relayer resigns after signing NEAR → Foreign transfers (setting themselves as `fee_recipient`) but before calling `claim_fee`, the fee portion of every such transfer is permanently locked in the bridge contract. The `pending_transfers` entries are never removed, `locked_tokens` accounting is never corrected, and the fee tokens cannot be recovered by any party without a contract upgrade.

This matches the allowed impact: *"Permanent freezing, irrecoverable lock, or unclaimable settlement of user or protocol funds in bridge, token, or vault flows."*

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Relayers are economically incentivized to resign and reclaim their stake (1,000 NEAR minimum) once they stop operating. The protocol explicitly supports resignation via `resign_trusted_relayer`. A relayer who processes many NEAR → EVM transfers, then resigns to recover their stake, will naturally have unclaimed fees for transfers that were signed but whose foreign-chain finalization proof has not yet been submitted. This is a routine operational sequence, not an edge case.

The DAO revocation path (`reject_relayer_application`) is also realistic: the DAO can revoke an active relayer at any time, immediately stranding all pending fees.

---

### Recommendation

Remove the `#[trusted_relayer]` guard from `claim_fee`. The function's security does not depend on the caller being currently trusted — it depends on the cryptographic proof binding the `fee_recipient` to the specific transfer. The `claim_fee_callback` already enforces `fee_recipient == predecessor_account_id` via proof verification, making the `#[trusted_relayer]` check redundant for security and harmful for correctness.

Alternatively, record the `fee_recipient` at `sign_transfer` time inside the `pending_transfers` entry, and allow any caller to submit the proof as long as the proven `fee_recipient` matches the stored one.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Relayer R is trusted. They call `sign_transfer` for 50 NEAR → EVM transfers, setting themselves as `fee_recipient` in each signed payload.
2. Users submit those signed transfers on the EVM chain. The EVM bridge emits 50 `FinTransfer` events, each embedding R's address as `fee_recipient`.
3. R calls `resign_trusted_relayer` to recover their 1,000 NEAR stake. `is_trusted_relayer(R)` now returns `false`.
4. R attempts to call `claim_fee` with a proof of any of the 50 `FinTransfer` events.
5. The `#[trusted_relayer]` guard rejects the call because R is no longer in the trusted set.
6. No other account can call `claim_fee` for these transfers because `claim_fee_callback` requires `fee_recipient == predecessor_account_id`, and the proof binds `fee_recipient` to R's address.
7. All 50 `pending_transfers` entries remain in storage indefinitely. The fee tokens (the difference between the locked amount and the amount delivered to the recipient) are permanently frozen in the bridge contract. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3)
