Audit Report

## Title
Missing Deadline Check in All Four `MetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder` Liquidity Entry Points Allows Stale Execution at Unfavorable Prices — (`metric-periphery/contracts/MetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder.sol`)

## Summary
`MetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder` exposes four public liquidity entry points — two overloads of `addLiquidityExactShares` and two overloads of `addLiquidityWeighted` — none of which accept or enforce a `deadline` parameter. By contrast, `MetricOmmSimpleRouter` calls `_checkDeadline` as the first statement in every swap path. A pending liquidity transaction can therefore be held in the mempool and mined arbitrarily far in the future at a pool price the user never intended, causing immediate impermanent loss on the deposited principal.

## Finding Description
`_checkDeadline` is defined in `MetricOmmSwapRouterBase` (L91–94) and is called at the top of all four swap entry points in `MetricOmmSimpleRouter` (L68, L93, L131, L155). `MetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder` inherits only from `IMetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder` and `PeripheryPayments` — not from `MetricOmmSwapRouterBase` — so it has no access to `_checkDeadline` and no equivalent guard anywhere in its public surface.

The four unprotected entry points are:
- `addLiquidityExactShares(address,address,uint80,LiquidityDelta,uint256,uint256,bytes)` (L56–68): no deadline, no price-range guard.
- `addLiquidityExactShares(address,uint80,LiquidityDelta,uint256,uint256,bytes)` (L71–81): no deadline, no price-range guard.
- `addLiquidityWeighted(address,address,uint80,LiquidityDelta,uint256,uint256,int8,uint104,int8,uint104,bytes)` (L88–116): has `_validateBinAndBinPosition` but no deadline.
- `addLiquidityWeighted(address,uint80,LiquidityDelta,uint256,uint256,int8,uint104,int8,uint104,bytes)` (L123–149): has `_validateBinAndBinPosition` but no deadline.

The `_validateBinAndBinPosition` guard (L263–286) checks whether the pool cursor is within a user-supplied bin/position window at execution time. This is a price-range guard, not a time guard. A user who specifies a wide cursor window (or any window that still contains the cursor after a large price move) receives no protection. The probe-then-pay flow in `addLiquidityWeighted` runs the probe at whatever price the pool holds at execution time (L106–115, L139–148), computes the scale factor at that stale price, and deposits tokens at that price. The `maxAmountToken0`/`maxAmountToken1` caps enforced in the callback (L165–167) limit total token spend but do not protect against bad-price composition: a user who sets generous caps to accommodate normal slippage will still have the full capped amount deployed at a stale price.

## Impact Explanation
An LP submits a liquidity transaction at price P. The transaction is delayed in the mempool. By the time it is included, the pool price has moved to P′. For `addLiquidityWeighted`, the probe runs at P′, the scale factor is computed at P′, and the user's tokens are deposited into bins priced at P′. When the market price reverts toward P, the position immediately suffers impermanent loss proportional to the price deviation — a direct reduction of the LP's redeemable principal. For `addLiquidityExactShares`, the user's fixed share amounts are deposited at whatever bin prices exist at execution time, with no recourse. The loss is bounded by the user's caps but can be substantial for large cap values or large price moves. This constitutes a direct loss of user principal, qualifying as Medium severity under Sherlock thresholds.

## Likelihood Explanation
Any pending transaction in a public mempool is subject to this condition. No privileged access is required. Volatile markets, gas-price spikes, and MEV searchers who benefit from delayed LP entry all increase the probability. The trigger is ordinary network latency combined with price movement, making this a realistic and repeatable condition for any LP using this contract.

## Recommendation
Add a `uint256 deadline` parameter to all four public entry points of `MetricOmmPoolLiquidityAdder`. Since the contract does not inherit `MetricOmmSwapRouterBase`, either inherit it or duplicate the `_checkDeadline` helper (or extract it to a shared base). Call `_checkDeadline(deadline)` as the first statement in each function, before any pool interaction or validation:

```solidity
function addLiquidityExactShares(
    address pool,
    address owner,
    uint80 salt,
    LiquidityDelta calldata deltas,
    uint256 maxAmountToken0,
    uint256 maxAmountToken1,
+   uint256 deadline,
    bytes calldata extensionData
) external payable override returns (uint256 amount0Added, uint256 amount1Added) {
+   _checkDeadline(deadline);
    _validateOwner(owner);
    ...
}
```

Apply the same pattern to both `addLiquidityWeighted` overloads and the second `addLiquidityExactShares` overload.

## Proof of Concept
1. ETH/USDC pool price: 1 ETH = 2 000 USDC. User calls `addLiquidityWeighted` with `maxAmountToken0 = 4 000 USDC`, `maxAmountToken1 = 2 ETH`, cursor bounds wide enough to cover ±50% price movement.
2. Transaction sits in the mempool. ETH price moves to 3 000 USDC. The cursor is still within the user's wide bounds — `_validateBinAndBinPosition` does not revert.
3. Transaction is included. The probe (L106–115) runs at the 3 000 USDC/ETH price, returning `need0 = 6 000 USDC`, `need1 = 2 ETH`. `_scaleWeightsToShares` computes `scale = min(4000/6000, 2/2) = 0.667`. User deposits 4 000 USDC + 1.333 ETH at the 3 000 USDC/ETH price.
4. ETH price reverts to 2 000 USDC. The position's redeemable value is now less than 4 000 USDC + 1.333 ETH at 2 000 USDC/ETH due to impermanent loss — a direct reduction of the LP's principal with no recourse.
5. A Foundry fork test can reproduce this by: deploying the pool at price P, submitting the `addLiquidityWeighted` call, warping `block.timestamp` forward and moving the pool price to P′ via a swap, then executing the queued call and measuring the LP's redeemable value against their deposited value.
