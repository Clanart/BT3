### Title
Time-Averaged L1 Gas Price (TWAP Analog) Causes Stale `l1_gas_price_fri` Committed in Block Hash During Volatility - (File: `crates/apollo_l1_gas_price/src/l1_gas_price_provider.rs`)

### Summary
`L1GasPriceProvider::get_price_info()` computes a simple arithmetic mean over `number_of_blocks_for_mean` (300) L1 blocks, offset by `lag_margin_seconds` (600 s) — a direct TWAP analog. During L1 gas price volatility, this mean significantly misrepresents the true current price. The resulting `l1_gas_price_fri` is committed into the block hash and used for all transaction fee accounting. Because both proposer and validator call the same `get_l1_prices_in_fri_and_wei()` path backed by the same mean, the 10 % margin check in `is_block_info_valid()` passes on the wrong value, so the stale price is accepted and finalized.

### Finding Description

**Root cause — mean computation in `get_price_info()`:**

`get_price_info()` selects the window `[first_index, last_index)` such that the newest included block has `timestamp ≤ requested_timestamp − lag_margin_seconds`. It then sums `PriceInfo` over that window and divides by `actual_number_of_blocks`:

```rust
let price_info_out = price_info_summed
    .checked_div(actual_number_of_blocks)
    .expect("Actual number of blocks should be non-zero");
``` [1](#0-0) 

In production (`number_of_blocks_for_mean = 300`, `lag_margin_seconds = 600 s`): [2](#0-1) 

At ~12 s/L1 block, 300 blocks ≈ 60 minutes of history, plus the 600 s lag. The effective price lag is **~70 minutes**. During a sharp L1 gas price spike the mean stays near the pre-spike level for the entire averaging window.

**Propagation to block hash:**

The mean `base_fee_per_gas` flows through `get_l1_prices_in_fri_and_wei_and_conversion_rate()`: [3](#0-2) 

It is converted to FRI via `L1PricesInFri::convert_from_wei()` and placed in `ProposalInit.l1_gas_price_fri`. That value is then committed into the block hash through `gas_prices_to_hash()` → `calculate_block_hash()`: [4](#0-3) 

**Why the 10 % margin check does not protect:**

`is_block_info_valid()` calls `get_l1_prices_in_fri_and_wei()` with the proposed timestamp and compares the result against the proposer's value within `l1_gas_price_margin_percent = 10 %`: [5](#0-4) 

Both proposer and validator share the same `L1GasPriceProvider` implementation and the same 300-block mean. They therefore compute the **same stale value**, so the margin check passes and the wrong price is accepted.

**Compounding factor — ETH→STRK oracle fallback:**

`EthToStrkOracleClient::eth_to_fri_rate()` additionally falls back to the cached rate from `quantized_timestamp − 1` (up to one full `lag_interval_seconds = 900 s` older) whenever the current query is not yet resolved: [6](#0-5) 

This stale conversion rate multiplies the already-stale wei price, compounding the error in `l1_gas_price_fri`.

### Impact Explanation

The corrupted value is `l1_gas_price_fri` (and `l1_data_gas_price_fri`) in every block produced during a period of L1 gas price volatility. This value is:

1. **Used for all transaction fee accounting** — passed as `BlockInfo.gas_prices.strk_gas_prices.l1_gas_price` to the blockifier for every transaction in the block.
2. **Committed into the block hash** — via `gas_prices_to_hash()` → `calculate_block_hash()`, which is the hash anchored to L1.

During a gas price spike the sequencer undercharges for L1 gas (bad debt); during a crash it overcharges users. This matches: *"Critical. Incorrect fee, gas, bouncer, resource accounting, refund, balance, or L1 gas price effect with economic impact."*

### Likelihood Explanation

L1 gas price spikes of 5–20× within minutes are historically common (NFT mints, DeFi liquidation cascades).
